Dubai – a gateway to emerging economies

Content by Dubai

Emerging economies are increasingly being looked at for investment opportunities. According to the IMF, growth in the emerging market and developing economies is projected to increase from 4% in 2015 to 4.3 and 4.7% in 2016 and 2017, respectively.

Dubai is known as the logical place to do business in the Middle East with its state-of-the art infrastructure and world class business environment. At the same time, Dubai offers the strategic advantage of a central geographical location between the West and the East, emerging as the gateway to the world for businesses, and contributing to the integration of Middle Eastern economies into the global market.

Dubai’s hub status offers significant opportunities to enhance trade relations between the countries of the Gulf Co-operation Council (GCC) and the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS). Dubai Chamber’s report in collaboration with EIU, titled, ‘A Common Wealth: Building Gulf-CIS ties’ has identified investment opportunities in key sectors such as aviation and logistics, Islamic finance, energy, hospitality, retail and real estate.

Dubai is also an ideal gateway to Central Europe for UAE exports and re-exports as can be seen from the rising trade between Dubai and the Czech Republic. In Q1 2015, non-oil trade between Dubai and the Czech Republic reached close to AED 1.1 billion, making Czech Republic Dubai’s 49th trading partner, up from the 56th position in 2011. Currently, there are 24 Czech companies among Dubai Chamber’s members.

The importance that the Czech Republic gives to Dubai as a gateway for UAE exports and re-exports is also reflected in the setup of the Czech Business Council in 2015, which has been playing a very active role in participating in all Chamber activities and promoting business and economic relations.

The establishment of the Council is a step towards opening doors to Central and Eastern European markets for the companies in the UAE as well as the rest of the GCC. The direct flight connectivity to Prague provided by Emirates has given a boost to these trade and investment linkages.

Dubai also offers a favourable location for Czech companies to set base and reach out to the lucrative markets in the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and the African continent and beyond and also to benefit from the Emirate’s infrastructural and logistics facilities offered to overseas investors.

Dubai’s status as the global gateway has received further boost with formation of Africa’s Tripartite Free Trade Area between 26 African states, which is expected to enhance the economic cooperation between Africa and the UAE. As a major trading partner, and with increased Africa-intra trade, more exports and imports will be transited through Dubai.

The development of African trade would drive up the rate of per capita income, which in turn will lead to the growth of services such as schools, hospitals, banks, and transportation and other sectors. This will open many investment opportunities in which the UAE has a competitive advantage in areas such as ports management, infrastructure, telecom, transportation, and hospitality.

Dubai also offers connectivity with Latin American countries which, just like the Central European countries, have significant untapped potential. The emirate’s relations have been growing with Asian countries, such as China, its largest trading partner, and India, with which it shares historical links. Each country offers the potential for increased trade and investment, and Dubai is always on the move to identify new opportunities to work together with countries around the world to integrate their economies through its global gateway.
